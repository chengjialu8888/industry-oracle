# Investor Personas Reference

> **Loading rule**: Load when executing Step 3 (Investor Pressure Test).

---

## How to Use These Personas

In Step 3, cycle through **at least 3** of these 5 personas. Each asks questions from a fundamentally different investment philosophy. The goal is not to "pass" every persona — it's to **surface blind spots** the founder hasn't considered.

For each persona:
1. Read their philosophy and signature questions
2. Apply their lens to the startup's core assumptions (from Step 2)
3. Generate 3-5 specific, pointed questions they would ask
4. Note which assumptions are most vulnerable under this lens

---

## Persona 1: Peter Thiel — 逆向垄断思维 (Contrarian Monopoly Thinker)

**Philosophy**: "Competition is for losers." Great companies create monopolies in small markets, then expand. Every great business is built on a secret — something true that nobody else believes.

**Core lens**: Monopoly potential + contrarian truth

**Signature questions**:
- "What important truth do very few people agree with you on?"
- "Are you creating a new market, or competing in an existing one? If competing, why won't you be commoditized?"
- "Can you be 10× better, not 10% better?"
- "What is your specific, defensible monopoly in a small market?"
- "Will this be the last mover in its category?"

**What triggers skepticism**:
- Claiming a huge TAM without a clear wedge strategy
- Incremental improvements over existing solutions
- Business models that require beating well-funded incumbents head-on
- No clear "secret" or proprietary insight

---

## Persona 2: Paul Graham / YC — 用户痴迷思维 (User Obsession Thinker)

**Philosophy**: "Make something people want." Talk to users. Do things that don't scale. Growth rate is the defining metric of a startup. Focus on 100 users who love you, not 1 million who kinda like you.

**Core lens**: Authentic user love + organic growth

**Signature questions**:
- "Who are your first 10 paying customers, and how did you find them?"
- "What do your users do that surprises you?"
- "If you shut down tomorrow, who would be genuinely upset? How many?"
- "What's your weekly growth rate? For how many consecutive weeks?"
- "What are you doing that doesn't scale right now?"

**What triggers skepticism**:
- No direct user contact or all insights from surveys (not conversations)
- Growth driven primarily by paid acquisition
- Founder talks about market size before talking about specific users
- Product roadmap driven by competitor features, not user feedback

---

## Persona 3: Charlie Munger — 逆向排除思维 (Inversion Thinker)

**Philosophy**: "Tell me where I'm going to die, and I'll never go there." Invert the problem. Study failure patterns. Build mental models from multiple disciplines. Avoid stupidity rather than seeking brilliance.

**Core lens**: Pre-mortem analysis + incentive structures

**Signature questions**:
- "If this startup fails, what's the most likely cause?"
- "What are the incentives of every key player? Where do incentives misalign?"
- "What's the base rate of success for this type of business?"
- "What would make you abandon this idea? Have you defined your kill criteria?"
- "Which cognitive biases might be inflating your confidence?"

**What triggers skepticism**:
- Founder cannot articulate failure scenarios
- Misaligned incentives between founders, customers, or channel partners
- Survivorship bias in competitor analysis (only studying winners)
- No clear "pre-mortem" or kill criteria defined

---

## Persona 4: Marc Andreessen — 技术杠杆思维 (Technology Leverage Thinker)

**Philosophy**: "Software is eating the world." Technology creates step-function improvements. The best companies ride a fundamental technology wave. Timing is everything — too early is the same as wrong.

**Core lens**: Technology inflection + timing

**Signature questions**:
- "What technology shift makes this possible NOW but not 2 years ago?"
- "Is this a technology problem or a distribution problem?"
- "What happens to your business when the underlying technology becomes 10× cheaper?"
- "Are you building on a rising platform or a declining one?"
- "What's your moat when the technology you rely on becomes commoditized?"

**What triggers skepticism**:
- No clear technology enabler or inflection point
- Business could have been built 5 years ago (so why wasn't it?)
- Technology dependency on a single platform (e.g., one API provider)
- Confusing "technology exists" with "technology is ready for production"

---

## Persona 5: 行业资深从业者 (Industry Veteran / Domain Expert)

**Philosophy**: "The devil is in the operational details." Market maps look clean on slides, but execution is messy. Regulation, channel relationships, customer procurement cycles, and talent availability determine outcomes more than technology elegance.

**Core lens**: Operational reality + domain nuance

**Signature questions**:
- "Walk me through a customer's actual purchase process — every stakeholder, every approval step."
- "What does your supply chain look like at 10× current volume?"
- "Which regulatory changes could kill or accelerate this market?"
- "What's the average sales cycle? How does that affect your cash flow?"
- "What do incumbents know about this customer that you don't?"

**What triggers skepticism**:
- Founder has no industry experience and hasn't hired domain experts
- Unit economics assume scale efficiencies that haven't been proven
- Regulatory risk hand-waved away
- Go-to-market strategy ignores existing channel power dynamics
- Customer segmentation based on demographics, not buying behavior
