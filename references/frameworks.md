# Analysis Frameworks Reference

> **Loading rule**: Load when executing Step 2 (Assumption Deconstruction) or Step 4 (Multi-round Debate).

---

## 1. 范式三态模型 (Paradigm Tri-State Model)

Classify the industry's current phase to calibrate analysis depth and risk framing.

| State | Signal | Implication |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| **Pre-paradigm** | No dominant design; multiple competing approaches coexist | Focus on technology bets, team pedigree, optionality value |
| **Normal science** | Clear winner; incremental improvements dominate | Focus on execution speed, unit economics, distribution moats |
| **Crisis / paradigm shift** | Anomalies accumulate; incumbents' frameworks stop working | Focus on first-principles reasoning, willingness to cannibalize |

**How to use**: In Step 2, tag each core assumption with the paradigm state it implicitly assumes. If the assumption only holds in "normal science" but the industry shows "crisis" signals, flag it as high-risk.

---

## 2. 需求验证五问框架 (Five Questions for Demand Validation)

Use sequentially to stress-test whether real demand exists.

1. **频率 (Frequency)**: How often does the pain point occur? Daily > weekly > monthly > yearly.
2. **强度 (Intensity)**: When it occurs, how much does it hurt? "Hair on fire" vs. "mild annoyance."
3. **现有替代方案 (Existing alternatives)**: What do people do today? If they do nothing, demand may be imaginary.
4. **付费意愿 (Willingness to pay)**: Have they paid for inferior solutions? Paying customers > free users > survey respondents.
5. **可触达性 (Reachability)**: Can you find and acquire these people at reasonable cost?

**Scoring**: Each question scores 1-5. Total ≥ 20 = strong demand; 15-19 = conditional; < 15 = weak.

---

## 3. JTBD — Jobs to Be Done

Strip away demographics; focus on the "job" the customer hires the product to do.

**Template**: When I ___[situation]___, I want to ___[motivation]___, so I can ___[expected outcome]___.

**Application in Step 2**: Rewrite each "target customer" assumption as a JTBD statement. If you can't fill the template concretely, the assumption lacks grounding.

---

## 4. Sean Ellis PMF 测试 (Product-Market Fit Test)

> "How would you feel if you could no longer use [product]?"

| Response | Benchmark |
|----------|-----------|
| Very disappointed | **≥ 40%** → PMF signal |
| Somewhat disappointed | Soft interest |
| Not disappointed | No fit |

**Application in Step 3 (Investor Pressure Test)**: If the startup claims PMF but cannot show this metric (or a proxy like NPS ≥ 50, or organic growth > 40%), investors will push back hard.

---

## 5. WTP 阶梯 (Willingness-to-Pay Ladder)

Map pricing power to value capture potential.

| Level | Signal | Example |
|-------|--------|---------|
| **Level 0** | Won't use even if free | No demand |
| **Level 1** | Will use if free, won't pay | Feature, not product |
| **Level 2** | Will pay, but only < cost | Subsidy-dependent |
| **Level 3** | Will pay ≥ cost, shops around | Commodity |
| **Level 4** | Will pay premium, low churn | Moat / brand / switching cost |
| **Level 5** | Will pay and evangelize | Network effect / cult product |

---

## 6. 波特五力 (Porter's Five Forces) — Condensed

Use to quickly assess industry structural attractiveness.

| Force | Key Question |
|-------|-------------|
| **Rivalry** | How many credible competitors? Are they rational or desperate? |
| **New entrants** | What's the barrier? Capital, regulation, network effects, talent? |
| **Substitutes** | Could a different approach obsolete the whole category? |
| **Buyer power** | How concentrated are customers? Can they switch easily? |
| **Supplier power** | Any critical input controlled by few suppliers (e.g., chips, data)? |

**Application in Step 4 (Debate)**: The "industry skeptic" persona should invoke Porter's forces to challenge structural assumptions.

---

## 7. 克里斯坦森颠覆理论 (Christensen's Disruption Theory) — Quick Test

Two disruption patterns to check:

| Pattern | Signal | Watch for |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| **Low-end disruption** | Incumbents over-serve mainstream; startups target "good enough" tier at lower price | The startup's "inferior" product improving faster than customer needs grow |
| **New-market disruption** | Startup targets non-consumers; creates new behavior | Adoption curve among people who never used the old solution |

**Red flag**: If a startup claims "disruption" but targets the same customers with a slightly better product at the same price, it's sustaining innovation — incumbents usually win those fights.
